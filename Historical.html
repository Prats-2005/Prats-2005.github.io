Explore the history of North Goa!
Aguada Fort

Famed as the biggest water reservoir in Asia, Aguada Fort is the crowning glory of Portuguese legacy in Goa. This fort is more than 400 years old and attracts tourists with its mesmerizing beauty and rich history.

Chapora Fort

Located 10 km away from the town of Mapusa, bewitching and vivid, Chapora Fort in Chapora Village has remarkably withstood the ravages of time. One only needs an eye to appreciate the beauty of this secluded ruined Fort.

Reis Magos Fort

Standing tall and proud overseeing and protecting the taluka of Bardez, this fort was one of the first bastions of the Portuguese rulers. The structure exudes majesty and grandeur and commands respect and awe.

Aguada Jail

The Fort Aguada was converted into a jail during the Salazar administration, some say to house his political opponents. Dozens of peaceful protesters demanding that Goa be handed back to India, found themselves imprisoned here.

Fort Tiracol

This ancient and imposing fort on the river Tiracol is perhaps the best preserved and most visited fort in Goa. This is partly due to the Fort having been converted into an exclusive hotel.

Explore the history of South Goa!
Cabo de Rama Fort

Deriving its name from the Hindu Epic the Ramayana, this ancient fort far predates the Portuguese rule in Goa, making it one of the oldest forts in Goa, guarding the mouth of the Sal River

Palácio do Deão

The Palácio do Deão is situated,on the banks of the Kushavati River. A Palatial house, built in an unusual style of architecture and has an interesting blend of Hindu and Portuguese cultures.

Chandor village

This settlement, which includes a fort and a temple within its citadel. A renowned harbour and a powerful metropolis, Chandor in antiquity was where trade and commerce thrived.

Juve Fort

Juevem is rich in historical heritage and natural beauty. There is a fort situated ona hillock which was built in September 1668 known as the fort of Jua.

Lady of Conception

One of the most popular tourist attractions in Goa, the white sparkling church is majorly famed for its architecture and its flight of stairs that zigzag to the top.
