Visit the religious sites of North Goa!
Church of St. Francis of Assissi

The Catholic Church of Francis of Assisi is one of the examples of the majestic colonial architecture left over from the Portuguese rule in Goa. The church is located near Panaji, in Old Goa

Church of St Augustine

Standing high up on the Monte Santo at Velha Goa, the Church was Built by the Augustinian friars who arrived in Goa in 1587 & upon completion it was recognized as one of 3 great Augustinian churches

Shri Mahalasa Temple

This is one of the oldest temple situated in Mardol, with the Goddess Mahalasa narayani as it's deity. It is a very sacred and beautiful place.

Church of Our Lady Of Hope

In the heart of the coastal town of Candolim, stands a magnificent testament to history, spirituality and architectural elegance - Our Lady of Good Hope which carries a rich legacy.

Mahalaxmi Temple

The temple of Goddess Mahalaxmi has been in existence since at least the year 1413, when information about the rituals of the temple is found on stone plaques carved during the reign of Nanjan Gosavi Pratihast over Goa.

Visit the religious sites of South Goa!
Birla Radha Krishna Mandir

Spanning a vast expanse of 3.6 acres, the Radhe Krishna temple loacted at BITS Pilani Goa campus has elevated the state's cultural, social, and tourism landscape.

Tambadi Surla Mahadev Temple

The most ancient temple in the whole of Goa is Mahadev temple located at a place called Tambi Surla at Mollem. The temple itself was built in Jain style in the Twelfth century.

Mallikarjun Temple

The Shri Mallikarjun temple dedicated to Lord Mallikarjun, an incarnation of Lord Shiva is situated in the Sristhal village which is 7 km northeast of Chaudi in the Cancona taluka of South Goa. The shrine is known to be one of the oldest in Goa.

Rachol Seminary

Also known as the Patriarchal Seminary of Rachol, The Rachol Seminary is a major tourist attraction and the place is well-known for its religious significance.
